Oral Surgery

  1. Home
  2. Oral Surgery

Modern dentistry, appropriate surgical procedures and the use of local anesthesia, provides painless oral-surgical interventions with minimal trauma of surrounding tissues and rare postoperative complications. Oral surgery includes all surgical interventions in the oral cavity, such as tooth extraction, surgical extraction of implanted wisdom and eyebrows, extraction of backward roots, tooth root surgery in order to remove periapical processes (apicotomy), tumor and cyst removal, alveolar ridge leveling and other interventions as part of the preparation of soft and bone structures in the mouth for better prosthetic rehabilitation. Oral surgery includes implantation of dental implants, guided regeneration of solid and soft tissues, elevation of the maxillary sinus and other interventions in order to correct and correct irregularities and deformities of the face, jaw and teeth. Each tooth is valuable and the goal of modern dentistry is to take all the necessary measures to preserve your tooth.
